About Me

I am an Assistant Research Professor of Mathematics at Duke University, privileged to work with Professors Jianfeng Lu and Jonathan Mattingly. Previously, I had the honor of completing my Ph.D. at Johns Hopkins University under the guidance of Professors Yannick Sire and Fei Lu.

My research focuses on machine learning theory, with a particular emphasis on learning dynamics from data. This includes investigating interacting particle systems, quantum dynamics, and non-Markovian systems with memory. Additionally, I have a deep interest in data assimilation, inverse problems, and regularization.
